Research

Research Interests

My research agenda is grounded in construction practice and engineering education. I am especially interested in how digital tools, organizational systems, and competency-based training can improve project performance and workforce development under high-pressure conditions.

Construction Management and BIM

I study BIM-supported coordination, schedule control, quality and safety management, and owner-contractor interfaces across the construction project lifecycle.

Safety Training and Workforce Competence

Building on my MBA focus in human resource management, I examine competency-based safety training, blended learning, and work-learning conflict in engineering project teams.

Engineering Education

I am interested in practice-oriented teaching methods for construction technology, surveying, innovation, and entrepreneurship in vocational higher education.

Geotechnical and Foundation Performance

My current technical work includes PHC pipe-pile settlement verification using field static-load response and calibration of Duncan-Chang model parameters.

Current Research Question

How Can Project Teams Learn Under Pressure?

I am investigating how demanding engineering schedules create work-learning conflict and how competency-based blended learning systems can be reconfigured for project teams.

Approach
Mixed methods
Context
High-pressure engineering project teams
Outcome
Training-system design and competence development
